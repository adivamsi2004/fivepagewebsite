The studio

Two people, one press, since 2015

Marrow & Type started as a wedding-invitation side project and slowly became a full-time studio. Here's how that happened, and who's actually running the press.

Our story

It started with a broken press and a bad quote

In 2015, a friend's wedding invitation quote came back with a six-week lead time and a price that didn't match the flimsy paper sample attached. We'd just picked up a rusted tabletop proofing press at an estate sale for parts. Three weekends of cleaning it up later, we printed that invitation ourselves.

Word got around faster than expected. What was meant to be a one-off favor turned into a waitlist, then a spare room, then an actual studio lease. We still use that same press today — it's just no longer held together with parts from three different machines.
