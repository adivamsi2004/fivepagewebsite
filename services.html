What we do

Design, print, and finish — start to finish

Some clients arrive with finished artwork ready to print. Others arrive with a napkin sketch. Either way, these are the services that get a project from idea to a stack of finished sheets.

Custom Typesetting

We hand-set or digitally compose your layout, choosing type families that actually exist as physical plates in the studio.

Letterpress Printing

One to four ink colors, run on our proofing press at a depth of impression you can feel with your thumb.

Foil Stamping

Metallic gold, silver, or copper foil, stamped separately from ink runs for a crisp, reflective finish.

Embossing & Debossing

Blind (inkless) impressions for a subtle, tactile mark — often paired with a single ink color elsewhere on the sheet.

Paper & Envelope Sourcing

We stock cotton, recycled, and specialty stocks in-house, and can source anything unusual a project calls for.

Design Consultation

A sit-down (or video call) to work through layout, ink colors, and paper before anything goes near the press.

How a project moves

Five steps, roughly three weeks

  1. Consult

    We talk through what you're printing, your timeline, and your budget.

  2. Proof & Design

    You'll see a digital proof, then (for most orders) a physical press proof before we commit to a full run.

  3. Set the Plate

    Your approved design is etched onto a photopolymer or hand-set metal plate.

  4. Press Run

    Sheets are fed and printed one at a time, one ink color per pass.

  5. Finish & Ship

    Trimming, folding, and packing, then it ships or you pick it up from the studio.

Before you ask

Frequently asked questions

How far in advance should I order?

For weddings, 3–4 months before you need to mail invitations. For business stationery or art prints, 2–3 weeks is usually enough.

Can you match a Pantone ink color?

Yes — we mix ink to Pantone spot colors for every order. Bring a swatch or a Pantone number and we'll match it as closely as letterpress ink allows.

Do you print outside the categories on the Products page?

Often, yes. Menus, packaging, gig posters, and zine covers are all things we've printed before — get in touch and we'll tell you honestly if it's a good fit for letterpress.

What file format do you need for custom artwork?

Vector files (AI, EPS, or PDF with outlined type) work best. If you only have a sketch or a reference image, our design consultation covers building it into print-ready artwork.
